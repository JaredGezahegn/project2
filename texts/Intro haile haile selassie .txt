Haile Selassie was the Emperor of Ethiopia from 1930 to 1974.
 He is an important figure in Ethiopian and Rastafarian history. 
 

  Haile Selassie was born as Tafari Makonnen on July 23, 1892, in Ethiopia.
 He was the son of Ras Makonnen, a governor and general of the Ethiopian Empire.
 He became Emperor of Ethiopia in 1930 following the death of Empress Zewditu.
 His coronation name was Haile Selassie I, which means "Might of the Trinity" in Amharic.

 During his reign, Haile Selassie implemented several modernization reforms in Ethiopia.
 These included infrastructure development, educational reforms, and efforts to abolish slavery.

 Haile Selassie was a prominent supporter of Pan-Africanism 
and played a key role in the formation of the Organization of African Unity (OAU) in 1963, 
which later became the African Union (AU).
