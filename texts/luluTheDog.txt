Lulu was the beloved dog of Emperor Haile Selassie of Ethiopia, often seen as a symbol of loyalty and companionship. 
The dog gained notable recognition in both Ethiopian history and Rastafarian culture, 
where it is cherished as a symbol of the connection between the Emperor and his people. 
Lulu’s presence with Haile Selassie was more than that of a pet—it represented a bond that transcended the typical relationship between humans and animals, 
making the dog a cherished figure in Ethiopian and Rastafarian lore.
